The Metropolitan Development Index

The Metropolitan Develoment Index is comprised of three sub-indexs: economy, enviroment, and community. This allowed the largest 50 metropolitan statistical areas (MSAs) in the United States to be ranked based on their cumulative success in these areas. The economic index accounts for labor market conditions. Pollution, climate, and non-market amenities are measured in the environment index. “Environment” is applied to include both the natural world as well as humanity’s use of space. The community index captures elements of social and human capital as well as physical health and safety. The MDI is positively correlated with economic prosperity and individual’s desire to reside in areas that offer higher levels of quality of life.

The Expanded MDI

The original MDI was expanded to add a freedom sub-index. This measure accounted for econonmic freedom in the largest 39 MSAs. The measures included in this sub-index are: union coverage, minimum wage, taxation, and startup density.
